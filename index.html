reporter in chicago

I cover Muslim communities and civil liberties issues, migration, and inequity. I think a lot about how media interacts with religion, and how global religious literacy has affected interpretations of Islam in particular.

I use data analysis to bolster my reporting and the reporting of colleagues -- I lean heavily on Python for parsing data and JavaScript for front-end design work.

I've worked at the Chicago Tribune since 2014; I've been a senior journalist since 2019. Among other important issues, I've reported on

As part of my Master's project at the UC Berkeley j-school, I built Islam For Reporters. I graduated from j-school in 2014 with a specialization in Data Journalism, and from NYU in 2011 with degrees in Journalism and Middle Eastern Studies.
