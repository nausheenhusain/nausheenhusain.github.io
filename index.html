reporter and professor

syracuse, ny

I cover civil liberties issues, migration, and inequities. I focus on Muslim communities; I think a lot about how media interacts with religion, and how differences in global religious literacy affect interpretations of Islam in particular.

I use data analysis to bolster my reporting and the reporting of colleagues -- I lean heavily on Python for parsing data and JavaScript for front-end design work.

I am an assistant professor at Syracuse University's S.I. Newhouse School Of Public Communication, teaching journalism with an emphasis on data analysis, data visualization, underreported communities and civil liberties. Before this, I worked as a data reporter at the Chicago Tribune for seven years, and as a senior reporter since 2019. Over the past several years, I've reported on

  • Muslim Americans affected by the 2017 Muslim Ban;
  • struggling efforts in Illinois and nationally to implement BOP guidance to empty prisons and jails as much as possible during the global pandemic;
  • a surge of COVID-19 cases at the Marion federal prison facility, and the conditions at the prison, as reported to me by inmates and their family members;
  • COVID-19's unique effects on communities of color;
  • FBI surveillance on an Arab-American neighborhood of Illinois;
  • the controversial "Guant√°namo North" prison units in the Midwest that house mostly Muslim prisoners;
  • the uphill battle refugee agencies are facing under the Trump administration;
  • the legal back-and-forth of Trump's "Muslim ban";
  • pharmacy deserts in Black neighborhoods of Chicago
  • Islam-related terms and anti-Muslim hate crime data regularly used and misused by reporters;
  • and allegations of improper business contracts at an Oakland charter school system, which eventually had its charter revoked.

I am frequently asked to speak or teach about using data and FOIA skills to cover underrepresented communities, basic programming concepts and strategies, and the limits of using data in American journalism.

I graduated from the UC Berkeley Graduate School Of Journalism in 2014 with a specialization in Data Journalism, and from NYU in 2011 with degrees in Journalism and Middle Eastern Studies.
